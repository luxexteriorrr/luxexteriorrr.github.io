Around A Memory is an interactive, web-driven installation exploring Internet Decay and Digital Preservation. The project immerses the viewers in a metaphorical digital environment where interactions with the installation trigger the rate of the enviroment’s deterioration. The installation encapsulates a calculated timeline of the webpage decay, altering the readability, positionality, and the very existence of HTML elements, culminating in a unsusable/empty HTML document. Aimed at creative practitioners, anthropologists, and digital activists, the project highlights Internet Decay by critically engaging with the unseen phenomena and materialising it through an immersive, interactive experience.

Today we to talk about the walk. Suburban upbringing does not come without the sense of privilege for the ability to take a moment from the problems in urban hymns and not the one which Velvet Underground sang about, but more son of the ones that are present in the Metal Machine. The constant clattering, noice and essentially the main polluter ie the human.

Growing up the simple pleasure of finding the so-called peace within the park/nature or whatever we chose to call it was an escape. Closing a loop, the city expands, replacing the bark trees with concrete ones. Although the problems in urban living are not for what this piece is wrote for - today we talk about ambience.

Years later, when the hedonistic desire for the ambience turned into a formed habit, the absence and presence of the feeling becomes so apparent and obvious. You start looking for that same sense not just in the physical manifestations, but far more beyond. You learn to cherish that feeling, you start to see it in the places where perhaps it is not to be seen.

And then there’s the web — vast, silent, fragile. Once built for information, now cluttered with noise. But between the static and the scroll, there are still shadows of something slower. Interfaces that breathe. A hyperlink, like a path. A webpage, like a memory. The walk doesn’t end at the screen — it refracts through it. This is a trace of that walk.

《Chapter 1》

The air is crisp, and the pavement hums with the quiet rhythm of the city waking up.

Each step crunches lightly against stray leaves, and the faint scent of damp earth lingers from last night’s rain.

A bus sighs at the corner, exhaling a handful of sleepy commuters.

The sun is still undecided, flickering between clouds, casting shadows that stretch and dissolve.

I slow my pace, letting the world drift past—shop shutters rattling open, a cyclist gliding by, the soft murmur of a distant conversation.

For now, I exist between destinations, moving for the sake of movement.

Diary entry monday 24 of march

There’s a peculiar calm in this in-between space.

Not quite stillness, but a looseness—like time hasn’t fully begun yet.

The city is tentative, unsure whether to remain quiet or ramp into noise.

I feel like a ghost inside it.

Light pools in unexpected places: between car mirrors, under benches, behind the thin veil of my breath.

Someone across the street laughs into their phone, but the sound barely reaches me, like I’m under water.

I trace the edge of a crack in the sidewalk with my shoe, waiting for nothing in particular.

Everything seems paused, held in place by a thread of morning softness.

The digital world hasn’t caught up yet—no pings, no messages, no demands.

Just motion for the sake of remembering what it feels like to walk, to notice.

A city breathing in.

And maybe, for a moment, I’m allowed to breathe with it.

The scent of warm plastic and distant coffee machines lingers.

Nothing is urgent, but something is quietly ending.

Diary entry monday 24 of march

The dirt path twists between towering trees, their gnarled roots breaking through the soil like old bones.

Sunlight filters through the dense canopy in shifting golden patterns, and the air hums with the soft trill of unseen birds.

Each step disturbs a memory—leaves crunching, distant laughter, the scent of pine.

An abandoned bench sits crookedly beside the path, its wood softened by time.

I hesitate, tracing a hand along the worn surface.

Who sat here last?

What thoughts did they leave behind?

The wind rustles, nudging me forward.

Some places aren’t meant to be lingered in too long.

Raindrops tap against my hood, a soft percussive rhythm that blends with the distant hum of traffic.

The world is blurred—water streaking against streetlights, reflections stretching across wet pavement.

My shoes leave faint imprints that vanish as quickly as they appear.

A figure with an umbrella moves past me, faceless in the mist, footsteps fading into the quiet.

I breathe in deeply—the sharp scent of rain, the earth rich and alive beneath the city’s concrete skin.

Each step feels lighter, as if the rain is washing something away, leaving only the present moment behind.

The sky melts into bruised oranges and deepening blues, a slow-motion exhale of the day.

Streetlights flicker on one by one, casting pools of warm light on the empty sidewalk.

I pass a row of shuttered storefronts, their neon signs still humming faintly, as if reluctant to let go of the day.

The distant sound of laughter drifts from an open window, mixing with the rhythmic click of my footsteps.

I could turn back, retrace my steps, return to where I started—but the road ahead is painted in fading light, and for now, I keep walking.
